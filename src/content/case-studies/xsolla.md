## Why this engagement is different

Most Crypto work we see starts with a blockchain team trying to ship to a consumer audience. Xsolla is the opposite: a mature Web2 payments business with real scale, moving carefully into on-chain infrastructure. The engineering constraints are different — you don't get to break the existing business — and the organizational shape of the project is different too.

## What we focus on

- **High-throughput payments infrastructure** that behaves under load and under regulatory review
- **Blockchain data** pipelines that stay coherent across chain reorgs and Layer 2 state updates
- **ML** where it moves the needle on fraud, routing, or pricing — not where it's a demo
- **Domain translation** between the Web2 payments world and the Crypto primitives teams they're building alongside

## What "digital backpacks" actually means

Cross-game asset ownership is the headline. Underneath it is a data model that has to handle millions of asset moves per day, resolve into many wallets and many games, and stay audit-ready. That's the problem we're helping solve.
